## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do you install luxury vinyl plank (LVP) flooring?

Yes, LVP installation is one of the most common jobs we do here at Rauen Flooring. Whether it's a living room, kitchen, bedroom, or a whole condo, we handle it all. That includes standard plank layouts and herringbone patterns. If you're replacing old carpet with LVP, we take care of the rip-out and prep work too. Just let me know the areas you're looking at and I can get you a quote.

### What is a float charge and why is it on my estimate?

Great question, and I get this one a lot. A float charge covers the cost of leveling your subfloor before we install. If your subfloor has dips, humps, or uneven spots, we have to pour a self-leveling compound to flatten everything out. Without it, your new flooring won't sit right, you'll get gaps, clicking, or boards that rock when you step on them. The cost depends on how much leveling is needed. I know it can feel like an unexpected line item, but it's one of those things that protects the quality of the whole job.

### Do you refinish hardwood floors?

Absolutely. If your hardwood is looking worn, scratched up, or faded, refinishing brings it back to life without the cost of a full replacement. We sand everything down, repair any problem areas, and apply fresh coats of finish. Bedrooms, hallways, living areas, we handle all of it. Reach out with some details about your space and I'll let you know what's involved.

### Do you install tile flooring?

Yes, we do tile installation. Bathrooms are one of the most common spots we tile, but we work in kitchens and other areas too. No job is too small. If you've got a bathroom that's around 60 square feet or a larger space, just send me the details and I'll put together a quote for you.

### Can you install transition strips between different flooring types?

For sure. Transition strips are what give your floors a clean, finished look where two different materials meet in a doorway or between rooms. Whether you've got three doorways or ten, we handle the install and make sure the transitions match your flooring as closely as possible. It's a small detail that makes a big difference in how the whole floor looks when it's done.

### How quickly can you start a flooring project?

It depends on our current schedule, but I always try to get jobs started as soon as possible. Once we agree on the scope and pricing, I'll look at the calendar and give you the earliest available date. Most of the time we can get things moving within the week. Just let me know what works for you and we'll find a time.
